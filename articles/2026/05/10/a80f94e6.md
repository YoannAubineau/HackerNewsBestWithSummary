## Résumé de l'article

Apple et Google déploient progressivement des systèmes d'attestation basés sur le matériel (vérification de l'intégrité du périphérique via le fabricant) pour contrôler l'accès à leurs services respectifs. Ces technologies permettent aux entreprises de certifier la nature et l'état des appareils utilisateurs.

- Google propose la Play Integrity API et Apple propose App Attest pour vérifier l'intégrité des appareils avant d'autoriser l'accès aux applications
- Apple a étendu cette approche au web via Privacy Pass, et Google envisage de faire de même
- Les deux géants convainquent progressivement d'autres services d'adopter ces systèmes d'attestation matérielle

## Discussion sur Hacker News (750 commentaires)

**Avis positifs** :
- Hardware attestation effectively concentrates power in the hands of Apple and Google, enabling them to lock out competition and alternative operating systems like GrapheneOS despite superior security records, exemplifying how monopolies weaponize security features for market control
- The current implementation of attestation (particularly Google's Play Integrity) is demonstrably not about security since it certifies unpatched devices with known vulnerabilities while rejecting more secure alternatives, proving the real motive is enforcing Google Mobile Services licensing requirements
- Governments including the EU are complicit in enabling this monopoly by adopting attestation-dependent systems for digital IDs, payments, and age verification, effectively outsourcing democratic sovereignty to US tech companies and creating single points of failure for critical services
- Remote attestation enables mass surveillance and deanonymization by tying all digital activities to attested hardware controlled by corporations, destroying the ability to maintain privacy or operate outside corporate-approved ecosystems
- The lack of interoperability forces users into choosing between device freedom and accessing essential services like banking, government portals, and social platforms, creating a new form of digital segregation based on approved hardware

**Avis négatifs** :
- Hardware attestation serves a legitimate technical purpose in preventing fraud and abuse by enabling services to verify they're communicating with genuine users on secure devices, not bots or compromised systems conducting fraud at scale
- The problem is not attestation technology itself but rather how specific companies implement it; properly designed systems using zero-knowledge proofs or blind signatures could provide human verification without enabling surveillance or monopolistic lock-in
- Banks and services have genuine security needs to reduce fraud losses that cost billions; solutions requiring users to carry separate physical authenticators or accept high fraud rates are less convenient and don't actually solve modern bot and AI abuse problems
- Critics overgeneralize about attestation being inherently evil when the technology enables beneficial use cases like verifying medical devices, securing supply chains, and protecting critical infrastructure from tampering
- Alternative approaches to proof-of-humanity are either unproven at scale, don't address the stated security requirements, or require user education and behavioral changes that evidence suggests won't materially reduce fraud compared to technical controls
